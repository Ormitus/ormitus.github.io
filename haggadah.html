The Project

A Tradition
of Designers

This year I had the distinct honor of participating in the 13th edition of the Asufa Passover Haggadah. Each year the project invites designers from all over the country to individually design a double page spread for the traditional text.

I was thrilled to be chosen to design the artwork for the Blood, fire, and pillars of smoke spread — one of the most iconic and visceral passages of the Seder night. It was an absolute pleasure to take part in this unique and creative tradition alongside other talented creators.

The Tradition

Thirteen Years
of Ink

The Asufa Haggadah is a beloved annual project in the Israeli design community. Each edition brings together a fresh cohort of designers — each assigned a different passage from the traditional Passover text — resulting in a wildly varied, deeply personal collective book.

For my spread, I drew from the raw, urgent energy of the passage itself. The words blood, fire, and pillars of smoke called for collage, stencil, and chaos. The result is a spread that feels pulled from a protest wall — reverent in subject, rebellious in form.
