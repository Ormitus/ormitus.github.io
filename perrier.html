The Brief

In Brief

130 years of sparkling French heritage — brought to life for a new generation of Israeli consumers.

The sparkling water brand Perrier was founded in 1898 in France and originates from a natural spring in the town of Vergèze. Over the years, Perrier has become a global icon — carrying with it the power of naturally carbonated mineral water and the feeling of elegance and sophistication of French culture and chic.


The brand commissioned a full strategic and creative overhaul: repositioning Perrier in the Israeli market to expand beyond its niche urban base, bridging the tension between French luxury heritage and accessible, everyday refreshment — without losing what makes it iconic.

Campaign Objectives

01 — Increase brand awareness beyond city limits; grow digital engagement and exposure.

02 — Differentiate and position Perrier as a lifestyle and chic icon that is accessible and speaks to everyone.

Challenge 01
Niche Marketing
Marketing activity that suits a relatively specific, urban-local market segment — based on typical codes and places. The brand struggles to reach audiences outside major cities.
Challenge 02
Soda Awareness
"Branding" sparkling water as old-fashioned, with consumer preference shifting toward sweetened carbonated drinks over natural soda.
Challenge 03
Personal Size
Perrier's flagship product is personal-sized — while many consumers seek family-sized solutions for everyday use, limiting purchase frequency.
Challenge 04
Price Perception
High price tag compared to competitors on the shelf — up to 30% price difference — making it a harder sell outside fine dining and premium contexts.
02
Market Research

Reading the Market

A growing category, a fragmented competitive landscape, and a clear white space for premium-accessible positioning.

Category Growth
Average annual growth of ~6–7% through 2030, with growing demand for natural products without artificial additives. The sparkling water market is valued in the billions and is in a continuous upward trend.
Consumer Shift
A marked shift from sweetened drinks to sparkling water; rise in demand for zero-calorie products and healthy alternatives to sweet carbonated beverages. Perrier is uniquely positioned to own this shift.
Experience Demand
Growing demand for drinks that provide experience and content. Consumers look for products that symbolize quality and can feel like a gift — something special even in everyday moments.
Sustainability
Rise in ecological awareness. Consumers prefer products with sustainable packaging and brands that demonstrate environmental responsibility — an area where Perrier's glass bottle is a natural asset.
Geographic Expansion
The Israeli market is changing and becoming more health-conscious and premium-oriented beyond large city centers. High potential to expand to less central regions and reach audiences seeking healthy but not overly expensive products.

Key Messages

Perrier Sparks Connections

Three core messages that span every touchpoint — from fine dining to a corner kiosk.

Nothing Extra.
Yet Extraordinary.
Natural water, sparkling in exactly the right measure, without pretension. Perrier doesn't need to be sophisticated to be an icon. It was born from a 130-year tradition, without changing, and still manages to be the most current thing there is. Proof that luxury doesn't need to announce itself.
Classic
by Nature.
Perrier was born in nature, deep beneath the earth, but lives everywhere — from a quiet morning in the park to a cocktail bar after midnight. It connects the primordial nature of mineral water with the most dynamic moments of modern life. Wild and balanced, accessible and luxurious, natural but always in motion.
Link the
Unexpected.
Perrier lives in the tension between different worlds — tradition and innovation, nature and urbanity, simplicity and extraordinary. When the most unexpected things meet, something new is created. Perrier brings the spark that connects past and future, a small moment to a special moment. Why choose when you can have both?
