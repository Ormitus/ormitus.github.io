01
Brand Story

The End of the
Compromise

Tel Mond finally has its first real cocktail bar. 4.8 is built for the neighborhood — not for anyone else.

For decades, the residents of Tel Mond have had a simple choice: drive to the city for a drink, or stay home. 4.8 is the end of that compromise.


We are the first true cocktail bar in the neighborhood — a milestone for the community and a "third place" for the locals. 4.8 was born from a return to basics. Founded by Noam, a former professional bartender who spent years in the high-tech industry, the bar represents a shift from the digital back to the manual.


It is a rejection of corporate sterility in favor of human warmth. We serve high-end cocktails, but we do it without the white gloves.

The Name

4.8

The bar's address — Dekel 48, Tel Mond. Named after the street it calls home, rooting the brand firmly in the neighborhood it was built for.

03
Logo

The Mark

Unapologetic. Industrial. Built to command space without needing to shout.

The 4.8 logo serves as the unapologetic anchor of the brand's visual identity. Set in the heavy, industrial 'Asphalt Black Condensed' typeface, the geometric mark is designed to command space without needing to shout.


Stripped of any unnecessary containers or borders, this solid, permanent structure stands in deliberate contrast to the brand's messy, handwritten 'ink' elements — perfectly capturing the effortless, unpretentious vibe of the neighborhood.

The concept

Asphalt

Heavy, industrial, permanent — the solid foundation of the brand.

04
Color

The Palette

Three core shades. Intentionally grounded. Zero enthusiasm for neon.

The color palette of 4.8 is intentionally grounded and mature, perfectly balancing industrial weight with inviting warmth. It completely avoids "enthusiastic" or flashy neon colors — together, these colors quietly set the mood without demanding attention, perfectly executing the "unpretentious excellence" the brand stands for.
The Asphalt
#262626
A deep, rich charcoal rather than a harsh digital pure black. Urban edge — the heavy anchor for primary typography and logo.
The Canvas
#F7F5E9
A warm, unbleached off-white. Gives menus and backgrounds the analog, tactile warmth of raw paper — instantly communicating the "living room" vibe.
The Local Touch
#2E493B
A sophisticated deep moss green. Adds depth without being loud. A nod to the agricultural roots of the Sharon region.
05
Typography

Asphalt & Ink

A deliberate tension between stable structure and human spontaneity.

The typographic system of 4.8 is based on a deliberate tension between stable structure and human spontaneity. The heavy, dominant Asphalt font projects a blunt, unapologetic, industrial presence for the logo and main headlines.


To break this rigidity, we integrate handwritten fonts — Kiya Handwrite in English and Pil Kachol in Hebrew — used for cocktail names and personal side notes, giving the brand its soul.


Technical information like drink ingredients and prices is displayed in the clean, minimalist Helvetica Neue Light and Galil Light fonts. This combination creates typography that feels established and serious on one hand, yet completely eye-level and unpretentious on the other.

Menu Design — 'Zine Aesthetic

The Menu Concept

The menu design for 4.8 abandons the stiff, corporate brochure look in favor of an authentic, local 'zine' aesthetic. Embracing our "Not Enthusiastic" ethos, it features rough, hand-drawn illustrations of glassware rather than polished photography. The content acts as a conversation with the neighborhood — highlighting deeply personal touches like the signature "10 Years Too Late" cocktail (a witty nod to Noam's high-tech hiatus) and drinks named directly after friends and locals, like Adi and Itamar.

06
Art Direction

Disposable Camera Realness

Grainy. High contrast. Black and white. Shot like a great night out, not a product launch.

Rejecting the glossy, staged, and perfectly lit aesthetic typical of high-end mixology bars, we use grainy, high contrast black and white imagery that feels like it was captured on a disposable camera during a great night out. The focus is entirely on honest, candid moments — the texture of condensation on a cold glass, the blur of a bartender shaking a drink, genuine laughter between friends, the unglamorous reality of cracking eggs for a cocktail.

Documentary photography — flash, grain, real moments only

07
Design Language

Asphalt & Ink

Every touchpoint is built from the same tension — heavy and industrial, but hand-drawn and human.

Design Language

The design language of 4.8 heavily relies on asymmetrical, organic layouts — raw, black-and-white documentary photography as backdrop, with stark typographic contrasts. Instead of rigid, sterile grids, the posters and social media assets look like indie gig posters or local community flyers.

Illustrative Language

The illustrative language acts as the 'Ink' to our 'Asphalt.' Featuring quick, unrefined doodles of glassware, pouring hands, and the bar's physical exterior — messy, zine-like, completely human. It makes high-end cocktails feel accessible and distinctly local.
